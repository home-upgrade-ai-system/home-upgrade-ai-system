Independent Merchant Website Implementation Case

SJ Home Improvement

A real local-business implementation showing how a merchant can keep its own website, brand identity, storefront information, and customer-facing content while using a BB369TECH-supported service structure, photo-first request pathway, human-confirmation boundary, and future-ready merchant routing architecture.

Current implementation statusSJ Home Improvement primarily operates through its own independent website and customer channels. BB369TECH supports and documents the service presentation structure, customer request path, semantic organization, and merchant-routing design. This case does not claim that all SJ customer traffic or completed orders currently pass through the BB369TECH platform.

Business Context

A real local home-improvement business with a physical storefront and multiple service categories.

Business

SJ Home Improvement, an independent merchant with its own website and brand presence.

Location

Rosemead, California, serving surrounding San Gabriel Valley communities.

Primary Services

Windows, doors, cabinets, wardrobes, storage, measurement, and estimate review.

Merchant Routing Reference

sj-windows-001 is reserved for BB369TECH-supported customer intake and merchant delivery.

Implementation Structure

The implementation separates merchant ownership from platform support.

Merchant-Owned Website

The merchant keeps its own repository, website, branding, phone number, images, and local search identity.

Structured Service Presentation

Services are organized in clear customer language instead of technical product terms alone.

Photo-First Request Path

Customers can begin with real project photos so the business can identify repair, replacement, custom work, measurement, or estimate follow-up.

Human Confirmation Boundary

Final price, measurement, materials, installation, scheduling, service quality, and responsibility require human confirmation.

Future-Ready Merchant Routing

The merchant can connect to a merchant-specific BB369TECH intake route without giving up its independent website.

Platform Provenance

BB369TECH documents the implementation model without claiming ownership of the merchant’s brand.

Customer Request Flow

The intended structure reduces unclear calls and repeated explanations.

1
Customer finds the merchant’s own websiteThe merchant remains the visible business identity and local brand.
2

3

Implementation boundary: The independent merchant remains responsible for final service, licensing requirements, pricing, measurement, materials, scheduling, installation, fulfillment, customer communication, and professional responsibility. BB369TECH provides structure, intake support, routing, communication support, and implementation documentation.

A local-service platform does not need to replace every merchant website.

Businesses can keep their own domain, repository, customer relationships, and local brand recognition.

Platform value remains visible

BB369TECH contributes the service structure, request logic, routing model, customer-intake pattern, and human-confirmation boundary.
